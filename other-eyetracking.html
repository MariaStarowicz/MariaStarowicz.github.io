eye tracking research


using Tobii Pro eye tracker and Tobii Pro Lab I conducted a minor eye-tracking research. the study involved presenting participants with 4 sets of images, each set displayed for 10 seconds, followed by their brief description of what they saw in the images. moreover they were given a test to estimate their level of pessimism and optimism.

the aim of this study was to investigate whether someone's level of optimism or pessimism influences their perceptiveness, specifically to identify which image elements are more intriguing to pessimists. during the study, participants were asked to describe what they saw in the images to understand the words used by pessimists versus optimists. additionally, it was hypothesized that one group might spend more time describing positive or negative aspects they noticed in the images, while another group might omit describing certain image elements despite the eye-tracking data indicating they noticed them.

the optimism test was taken after the eye-tracking study, using the test created by Professor Martin Seligman, who is one of the most influential psychologists globally. professor Seligman is renowned for theories such as learned helplessness and is also the creator of the concept of positive psychology.
